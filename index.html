About me

I grew up in the foothills of Idaho learning as much as I could about music. After high school, I attended Berklee College of Music, where I further honed my skills and expanded my knowledge. I graduated in 2016 and moved to Los Angeles to work in the music industry, and after a couple of years in that field, I discovered a passion for coding.

In the 1970s, IBM told their recruiters to seek out musicians if they ran out of programmers. They did this because musicians have a number of qualities that lend themselves to the development of software and new technologies. Much like reading a score, we have an attention to detail while keeping the big picture in view, and we can work with others while serving the needs of a whole project.

Above all, the skill that has helped me in music and now in coding is persistence. Sometimes you feel like Han Solo in the Millennium Falcon and sometimes you feel like you couldn’t effectively program a coffee machine. The important thing is to keep learning, working, and moving forward. I bring this sort of work ethic and problem-solving ability to the table.
